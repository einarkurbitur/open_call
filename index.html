What is the PS Action Club?

Throughout history artists have been recording their hand and mind through their work. Pablo Picasso was filmed by Belgian director Paul Haesaerts while painting on glass in 1949. The glass painting registered the way the hand of the master produced his signature paintings.

Sol LeWitt created written set of instructions between 1969 and 2007. These instructions were never executed by LeWitt himself. This way every execution and interpretations of the written instructions will be different.

One year after LeWitt died and thereby ended his series of written instructions, another American artist named Cory Arcangel created a series of Photoshop Gradient works. The title of the Gradients was the Photoshop instruction that would execute the work.

The PS Action Club is a platform that collects Photoshop Action files. These files are a set of Photoshop instructions that can be run via this program. The action will one by one play out in front of your eyes. This way the hand of the artist is formed inside the Photoshop action.

How to view an action in Photoshop

1. Download the action you want to view

2. Double click on the action to open it in photoshop

3. Open up the action window by going to Window -> Actions

To change the playback speed, press the button in the top right corner of the action window and select "Playback Options...". From there choose step by step.

Press play and enjoy
